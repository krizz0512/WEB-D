William Blake


28 Broad Street
Carnaby Market,
London,England

The soul walks naked through the fire of its own desire and
calls the burning truth a sin only because the world fears light.
I saw angels bound by laws they did not write and men crowned kings
though blind to eternity. Where reason builds its cold and narrow
walls, imagination breaks them with a whisper

Energy moves through all living things,
unseen yet undeniable, while restraint is merely the shadow
cast by fear. Those who deny desire deny life itself,
mistaking obedience for virtue and silence for peace.
The mind that dares to wander beyond limits finds heaven not above, but within.
